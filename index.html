Dr. Haunani-Kay Trask

The Champion of Native Rights in Hawaii

Here's a time line of Dr. Haunani-Kay Trask's life:

  • 1949 - Born in San Francisco, California, but grew up on the Ko'olau side of the island of O'ahu in Hawai'i.
  • 1967 - Graduated from Kamehameha Schools.
  • 1972 - Graduated from her Bchelor's degree in Political Science at University of Wisconsin - Madison.
  • 1975 - Graduated from her Masters's degree in Political Science at University of Wisconsin - Madison.
  • 1981 - Graduated from her PhD in Political Science at University of Wisconsin - Madison. Her dissertation was revised into a book, Eros and Power: The Promise of Feminist Theory , published by the University of Pennsylvania Press in 1986.
  • 1984 - Eros and Power: The Promise of Feminist Theory.
  • 1993 - Act of War: The Overthrow of the Hawaiian Nation & From a Native Daughter: Colonialism and Sovereignty in Hawaii.
  • 1994 - Night Is a Sharkskin Drum.
  • 1997 - Establishment of Gladys Brandt Kamakakuokalani Center for Hawaii Studies.
  • 1999 - Light in the Crevice Never Seen.
  • 2002 - We Are Not Happy Natives, CD-ROM(2002).
  • 2017 - Hawai'i Magazine recognized Trask as one of the most influential women in Hawaiian history.
  • 2021 - July - dies of cancer at the age of 71.
  • 2021 - September - the Department of Philosophy at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa issued a posthumous apology to Trask for attacks she received from Mānoa philosophers in the past.
